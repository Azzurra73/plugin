The Mathematical Foundation

Built on the Fokker–Planck Equation

The Heraklit Strategy is built on the Fokker–Planck equation — one of the foundational equations of mathematical physics, introduced by Max Planck in 1914 to describe the time evolution of probability distributions in dynamic systems. The same mathematical structure underlies Albert Einstein's 1905 treatment of Brownian motion and the Black–Scholes option pricing model, awarded the 1997 Nobel Prize for Economic Sciences (Merton / Scholes).

Marcus Schmieke has developed an extended form of this equation that classifies the state of a dynamic system into two regimes: stable circulation, and unstable directional drift. Applied to financial markets, this classification produces the constructive / critical signal that drives the strategy.

No machine learning. No data mining. No optimisation on historical data. The mathematical threshold is derived from the equation itself.

A Regime Classification Approach

The Mother Equation describes probability density evolution through three terms: Ds (diffusion coefficient — noise and quantum-like uncertainty), Φ (tension functional — directional tendency/drift potential), and Ωs (circulation field — cyclic non-equilibrium dynamics). The Regime Parameter ε = ‖∇Φ‖ / ‖Ωs‖ classifies system state: when ε > 1 (drift-dominated), the system approaches critical transition — alert phase. When ε < 1 (circulation-dominated), the system is in healthy, stable dynamics.

The full 27-year figure is disclosed for long-term context and to demonstrate the strategy's behaviour across both favourable and unfavourable regimes. Reconciling Signal Identification with the 83.1% Drawdown. The strategy issued regime-change signals during all seven major market dislocations in the 27-year backtest period, with substantially different effectiveness across crisis types. V-shaped crises with rapid recovery (COVID 2020, Rate Shock 2022, China-Oil 2015-16, Volatility Q4 2018) were navigated with strong protective performance. The 83.1% maximum drawdown is concentrated in the Dot-Com window and reflects this structural characteristic of the strategy rather than a failure of regime detection. The strategy's regime-aware logic is most effective in environments where crashes are V-shaped and central-bank intervention is responsive, a pattern that has dominated markets in the post-GFC period.

The Strategy Developer

Marcus Schmieke, Dr. h.c.

Marcus Schmieke is the developer of the Heraklit Strategy methodology applied in the Heraklit Strategy ETI. He studied physics at the Universities of Hanover and Heidelberg before turning to independent research in the mathematical modelling of dynamic systems.

Over more than two decades, he has developed the Quantum Blueprint Formalism — a research programme centred on an extended Fokker–Planck equation — and has applied this framework to the analysis of regime transitions in financial markets and other complex dynamic systems.

He has authored more than ninety research papers in mathematical physics and organises the annual Transdisciplinary Symposium at Schloss Kränzlin, Germany.
