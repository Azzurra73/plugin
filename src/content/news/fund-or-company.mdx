Many promoters instinctively reach for a familiar vehicle — a company, an SpA, an
Ltd, an SA. It feels safe. But for anyone serious about raising and managing
third-party capital, a regulated fund wins on structure.

## The structural advantages

- **Clean onboarding.** Investors subscribe to fund units directly — no notarial
  deeds, no shareholders' meetings for each new investor.
- **Legal asset segregation.** Investor assets are segregated by law from the
  manager's estate and from other sub-funds, with independent depositary oversight.
- **Tax efficiency.** Investors are taxed on redemption in their home jurisdiction;
  no tax event at fund level.
- **Recognised governance.** Investment Committee, Risk Manager, Compliance Officer
  and depositary — the framework institutional LPs require.
- **Native economics.** Hurdle rates, carried interest and differentiated share
  classes are native to fund law and immediately enforceable.
- **EU passport.** One MFSA authorisation unlocks distribution to professional
  investors across all 27 EEA member states.

## The takeaway

The moment your ambition exceeds your own balance sheet, the company structure
becomes a ceiling. A regulated fund is the instrument designed, from the ground
up, to attract, protect and scale third-party capital.
