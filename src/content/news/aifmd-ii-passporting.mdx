AIFMD II — Directive 2024/927/EU — is now the operating framework for alternative
investment fund managers across the European Union. For promoters, the headline is
continuity with sharper expectations: delegation arrangements, liquidity management
tools and regulatory reporting all face a higher bar.

## Why an authorised platform matters more than ever

Standing up an AIFM from scratch under the new directive means absorbing the full
weight of the enhanced requirements — substance, risk management, compliance and
reporting — before a single subscription is accepted. Launching as a dedicated
sub-fund under Framont's MFSA-authorised umbrella shifts that burden onto an
infrastructure that is already compliant.

- **Delegation clarity.** Portfolio management can be delegated to the promoter
  within a documented, supervised framework.
- **Liquidity management tools.** Pre-defined LMTs are embedded at structure level.
- **Reporting.** MFSA filings, SFDR disclosures and investor reporting are handled
  by the platform.

## The practical takeaway

The directive rewards managers who operate inside robust, supervised structures and
penalises improvised ones. For most promoters, the fastest route to a compliant,
EU-passported vehicle is to plug into an authorised AIFM rather than become one.
