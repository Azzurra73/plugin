The Heraklit Strategy ETI is a scientifically grounded Exchange Traded Instrument
built on a regime-classification approach. It applies an extended form of the
Fokker–Planck equation to distinguish between two market states: stable
circulation, and unstable directional drift.

## How it works

The strategy monitors global data streams in real time and derives a single
regime parameter, ε. When circulation dominates (ε < 1), the system is in healthy,
investable dynamics. When drift dominates (ε > 1), the system is approaching a
critical transition — an alert phase.

- **No machine learning.** The mathematical threshold is derived from the
  equation itself, not optimised on historical data.
- **Listed and liquid.** The ETI is listed on a European exchange and tradable
  through standard brokerage accounts, accessible from €1,000.
- **Clear separation of roles.** iMaps ETI AG (Liechtenstein) issues the
  instrument and acts as market maker; Framont acts as Delegated Portfolio
  Manager under MFSA authorisation.
